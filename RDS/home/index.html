Pop art was first introduced in the 1960s, as an art movement used to glamorize everyday items. Initially pioneered by prominent artists such as Andy Warhol and Roy Lichtenstein, the use of unique materials and methods such as silkscreen printing and lithography pioneered the commercial use of art. While advertising before the Second World War was primarily targeted toward informing consumers about the features of the marketed product in question, the advent of pop art and the commission of artists turned everyday objects marketed to the masses into aesthetic and unique items that appealed to the desires of consumers.
